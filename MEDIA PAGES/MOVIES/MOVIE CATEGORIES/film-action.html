Extraction

2020

Director:

Sam Hargrave

Stars:

Chris Hemsworth, Bryon Lerum, Ryder Lerum

Synopsis:

The film follows Tyler Rake, a weary mercenary sent into the heart of Dhaka to rescue a kidnapped boy against impossible odds. I'm genuinely captivated by the film's commitment to technical precision, especially that iconic 12-minute "one-take" sequence. It's an incredibly immersive piece of filmmaking that makes the audience feel every hit and high-speed shifts. Chris Hemsworth brings a raw, physical intensity to the role that elevates the entire production into a modern action masterclass.

John Wick

2014

Director:

Chad Stahelski

Stars:

Keanu Reeves, Michael Nyqvist, Alfie Allen

Synopsis:

The narrative centers on a legendary retired hitman who is forced back into the underworld after a mobster's son steals his car and kills the puppy left to him by his late wife. I love this movie because it essentially redefined the genre by introducing "Gun-Fu", a rhythmic, balletic fusion of close-quarters grappling and tactical shooting. It's a stylish, minimalist revenge story that manages to build a fascinating secret society while maintaining a relentless, unstoppable pace.

The Gray Man

2022

Directors:

Anthony Russo Joe Russo

Stars:

Ryan Gosling, Chris Evans, Ana de Armas

Synopsis:

This story follows Sierra Six, a top tier CIA shadow operative who goes on the run after uncovering agency secrets, pursued by a sociopathic mercenary across the globe. I really appreciate how the film balances massive, colorful set pieces with a sharp, witty script. The chemistry between Ryan Gosling's stoic lead and Chris Evans unhinged villain makes the high stakes manhunt feel both dangerous and incredibly entertaining. It's a vibrant, fast-paced flex of a spy thriller.
